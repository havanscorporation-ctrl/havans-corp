Scenario-Based Papers

Scenario-based papers are designed to assess learners’ ability to apply school knowledge to real-life situations. Instead of testing memorization, these assessments focus on reasoning, interpretation, and decision-making.

Each paper presents realistic contexts such as business activities, farming, transport, data analysis, budgeting, and community problem-solving. Learners are required to analyze information, choose appropriate subject methods, and explain their reasoning clearly.

Key Features

  • Real-life scenarios aligned with learners’ environment
  • Integration of multiple topics in one problem
  • Emphasis on explanation, reasoning, and justification
  • Assessment of higher-order thinking skills

These papers prepare learners for modern competency-based assessments and equip them with skills needed beyond examinations.

Curriculum Alignment Guide

Havans Corporation resources are fully aligned with the approved secondary school mathematics curriculum. Each assessment item is mapped to specific curriculum strands, learning outcomes, and competencies.

Curriculum Strands Covered

  • Number & Computation
    Integers, fractions, decimals, ratios, percentages, indices, logarithms, and financial mathematics
  • Algebra
    Algebraic expressions, linear and quadratic equations, inequalities, sequences, and functions
  • Geometry & Measurement
    Plane geometry, triangles, polygons, coordinate geometry, mensuration, and trigonometry
  • Statistics & Probability
    Data collection, frequency tables, graphs, measures of central tendency, and probability
  • Problem Solving & Reasoning
    Application of mathematics to real-world and interdisciplinary contexts

Competencies Developed

  • Critical thinking and analytical reasoning
  • Communication of mathematical ideas
  • Decision-making using quantitative information
  • Independent and collaborative problem solving

This alignment ensures that learners are assessed fairly while meeting national education standards.
